Privacy Policy

Roblox Asset Browser  ·  Last updated: March 2026

Overview

Roblox Asset Browser is a browser extension that lets Roblox developers browse, upload and manage their assets directly from Chrome or Firefox. This policy explains what data the extension accesses and how it is used.

Data collected

The extension reads your Roblox session cookie (.ROBLOSECURITY) to authenticate requests to Roblox's official APIs on your behalf.

This credential is stored locally on your device using chrome.storage.local. It is never transmitted to any server other than Roblox's own API endpoints (*.roblox.com).

No other personal data is collected, stored, or transmitted.

How the data is used

The session cookie is used exclusively to:

  • Verify your Roblox identity
  • Fetch your assets and group memberships from Roblox's APIs
  • Upload files to your Roblox account
  • Archive assets on your behalf

Data sharing

Your data is never sold, shared, or transmitted to any third party. All network requests go directly from your browser to Roblox's official API endpoints. The extension has no backend server of its own.

Data retention

The session cookie is stored locally on your device until you log out of the extension or clear your browser storage. No data is retained on any external server.

Third-party services

The extension communicates exclusively with the following Roblox-owned domains:

  • users.roblox.com — authentication
  • groups.roblox.com — group membership
  • itemconfiguration.roblox.com — asset listing
  • thumbnails.roblox.com — asset previews
  • assetdelivery.roblox.com — audio playback and decal resolution
  • apis.roblox.com — asset upload
  • develop.roblox.com — asset archiving
  • auth.roblox.com — CSRF token

Children's privacy

This extension does not knowingly collect any information from children under 13. It is intended for use by Roblox developers managing their own accounts.

Changes to this policy

Any updates to this privacy policy will be reflected on this page with an updated date.
